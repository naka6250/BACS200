CHICKEN KATSU

Chicken Katsu, also known as chicken cutlet or panko chicken is a Japanese dish of fried chicken covered in panko bread crumbs and is generally served with tonkatsu sauce on the side, and served on top of a bed of rice. Chicken Katsu originated from Japanese plantation workers who moved to Hawaii and brought their traditions over to Hawaii, where it has grown to become a popular plate lunch food that is served today and one of them is the famous chicken katsu.
