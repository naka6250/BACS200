Hawaii Foodbank- Nonprofit Organization

Hawaii Foodbank is a nonprofit agency in the state of Hawaii that collects, warehouses and distributes mass quantities of both perishable and nonperishable food through approximately 200 charitable agencies on Oahu and Kaui.

In 1982, John White's vision of a local food bank was one step closer to reality when the Good Samaritan Law governing food donations passed the State Legislature. The law encouraged the donation of unmarketable products by protecting donors from liability except in cases of gross negligence or wanton acts. To our knowledge no person has ever become ill from dontated food bank products. In May of 1983, John, along with a well-connected local truck driver named Ron Keith, opened a small warehouse on Sand Island that became Hawaii Foodbank. All the equipment used was dontated by local companies and included two refrigerated containers, a flatbed truck and a three-ton forklift. By the end of their first year Hawaii Foodbank has grown exponentially over these years and is the reason they have been so successful when making an impact to the Hawaii community.

Their pledge to the community beguin in 1996, Hawaii Foodbank was rated Hawaii's #1 charity by Hawaii Business magazine. At that time, 94% of all donations fed the hungry. Today, that same percentage stands and is a testament to the financial strength and efficiency of their whole organization. Hawaii Foodbank is a certified member of Feeding American, which means they meet the following national standards:
  • The safe handling, receiving, storage, and distribution of food
  • The financial management and stability of the organization
  • Board governance
Hawaii Foodbank have also obtained partnerships with other nonprofit agencies like Aloha United Way and Kauai United Way. Being proud partners together and a member of the Voluntary Organization Active in Disasters, Hawaii Foodbank won statewide recognition for its contribution to the relief effort. Hawaii Foodbank was recognized as a key asset to disaster preparedness and response organization for the state of Hawaii.

Programs

Food 4 Keiki

In Hawaii, 2 in 6 keiki may not know where they will get their next meal. More than 54,000 keiki in Hawaii are facing hunger, getting the energy they need to learn and grow can be a daily challenge. Thats why Hawaii Foodbank has developed "Food 4 Keiki" program which is a group of programs that aim to provide nutritious food to improve our keiki's health and well-being. Kupuna Programs

The Kupuna Program is focused on the senior citizens and elderly people of Hawaii, considering Kupuna means grandparents in Hawaiian. But, the Kupuna Program offers Senior Food Box Programs which is also known as the Commodity Supplemental Food Program, CSFP. The CSFP distribute food boxes to improve the health of seniors with low income. The box will usually contain supplemental foods such as nonfat dry milk, rice, canned meat, fruits, and vegetables inside. Ohana Produce Plus

The Ohana Produce Plus Program serve families with low income, individuals with disabilities, the homeless, and working homeless individuals. For many people Ohana Produce Plus is their only source of fresh fruits and vegetables. As one lady shares her story about how her husband was working, but didn't make a steady income. She was pregnant, and at the time her mom was battling cancer. A month after their daughter was born, they were unable to receive food stamps and they wouldnt be standing today without the help of the Ohana Produce Plus Program.
